Since I was 5 years old, I have had a fervent love of computers and technology, bounded only by realities of economics and time. I would spend hours playing games on this beautiful computer my dad got in 2007. Get a peek into my life... 

--- 

### 2007 - 2008

I was hooked on to this computer I had, that just fasincated me everytime I used it. I would play Cricket 07, space shooters, roadrash and many more, every single day. All of my friends would come together every evening to go to the near by cyber cafe[^1] and play call of duty all night. 

### 2009 

This was when iPods were with everyone you see around. The hype was real. You really could store 8 gigabytes of music, videos and games which was magic. This was also when I first got on YouTube. Listening to Linkin Park and Akon's "Smack that" made my day. The iPod truly was revolutionary. After some time, I got on MySpace because I thought that was necessary in order to be a rapper and I so wanted to be one. lol. 

### 2010 

I think it was around December of 2010, that I played a game made by a senior at my school. He made an alien shooter using QBasic which was a pretty _basic_ language at the time. I wanted to make one too, so I asked him for help and I wrote my first line of code. I made a lot of stupid games and played around DOS with the help of [Pete's QBasic Site](http://petesqbsite.com/).

### 2011

Now. I started making games using [3D RAD](http://3drad.org/). I had a lot of fun making cool games, *Driving mania* had the hype, but I soon got bored of it as there wasn't much I could think of other than racing cars. Then I came across HTML and I started learning it with a friend of mine in 5th grade. We had a lot of fun making websites and this was also when I discovered Orkut. 

### 2012 

Me and my above mentioned friend started hacking GTA Vice City and other small games using Cheat Engine. Oh boy did we feel good! We started playing miniclip games and experimenting with our computers. 

### 2013 

All my time was spent on playing games and exploring the internet. I thought HTML was the pinnacle of computer programming until Python and Java hit me like a truck. This was the year I became very curious about programming. 

### 2014 

I was very interested in cars from a young age and this year, I had got a small remote controlled car as a gift. Not a day passed and I had broken it into pieces and was playing with its DC motor. That caught on to me. I asked my friends to get their RC cars and we broke all of them, bought a couple batteries and played around with the electronics and the LED strips. 

### 2015

We were all into electronics at this point so we started exploring other projects we could make using these materials. A friend, who was 20 at the time, suggested we make a pirate radio and we were a bunch of 13 year olds who didn't understand anything. We looked up and with the help of [this wired article on how to make a pirate radio](https://www.wired.com/2015/11/create-your-own-pirate-radio/), we made a working radio. This was a really fun project and we all enjoyed it although we broke it the same day. < damn kids >

### 2016 - 2017 

These two years, I looked into competitive programming, penetration testing, software engineering, Linux, open source projects and GitHub. I loved what I had found and I started fiddling with all of the above. I fell in love with hacking[^2] as a concept and I wanted to learn it. I started doing CTF's and sometimes I would break some programs on [crackmes](https://crackmes.one/) too. The rush I had when discovering a vulnerability was unexplainable.

### 2018 - 2019

I just continued with whatever I had my hands on. I mainly realised that computer science isn't just programming or just about machines. Its about solving problems and that change of perspective did me right. I got my Certification from Cybrary as a Certified Penetration tester although it was a brief introduction to the world of cybersecurity. I learnt more about cloud services like GCP and AWS and tinkered around IoT and Robotics using Raspberry Pi. 

### 2020

Ah the goddamn corona virus. This year was a roller coaster ride. -_-
